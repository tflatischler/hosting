The Mission

The sun is dying. Earth's last hope is one reluctant astronaut.

Dr. Ryland Grace wakes aboard the starship Hail Mary with no memory of who he is or why he's there. His two crewmates are dead. And he's 11.9 light-years from home.

Piece by piece, he remembers: the Astrophage, an alien microorganism feeding on Earth's sun, is driving the planet toward extinction. Ryland's mission — a one-way trip to Tau Ceti — is humanity's final gamble.

But when he discovers he's not alone in the void, everything changes. An alien from the 40 Eridani system, whom he calls Rocky, faces the same threat. Together, they forge an extraordinary partnership — part science, part trust, part friendship — to save both their worlds.

Key Characters
🧑‍🔬

Dr. Ryland Grace

Reluctant Astronaut

A molecular biologist turned middle school science teacher, thrust into a solo suicide mission to save Earth. Armed with wit, tenacity, and an obsession with the scientific method.

🪨

Rocky

Eridian Engineer

A spider-like alien from 40 Eridani, communicating through musical tones. Brilliant engineer whose species faces the same solar crisis. Ryland's unlikely best friend across the stars.

🌍

Eva Stratt

Project Leader

The formidable head of Hail Mary — given absolute authority by every nation on Earth. She'll do whatever it takes, no matter the moral cost, to save humanity from extinction.

Core Themes

What the story explores

Hard science and deep humanity, woven together with humor and hope.

I

The Scientific Method as Survival

At its heart, this is a love letter to science — observation, hypothesis, experiment, repeat. Ryland solves extinction-level problems the way he'd teach a classroom: one careful step at a time.

II

Friendship Without Borders

Ryland and Rocky share no language, no biology, no planet — yet they build one of fiction's most moving friendships. Communication and trust transcend every possible barrier between them.

III

Sacrifice and Moral Complexity

The novel never shies from hard questions. Stratt sends people on suicide missions. Grace was launched against his will. When does the greater good justify taking away someone's choice?

IV

Optimism in the Face of Doom

Against apocalyptic odds, the story insists on hope — not through naivety, but through the stubborn, iterative, collaborative work of solving problems together.
