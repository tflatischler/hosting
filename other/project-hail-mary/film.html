About the Film

From page to screen

The 2026 film adaptation brings Andy Weir's beloved novel to life with stunning practical effects and a deeply human performance from Ryan Gosling. Directors Phil Lord and Christopher Miller — known for their inventive approach to blockbuster filmmaking — chose to build the entire Hail Mary spacecraft as a physical set, avoiding green screens wherever possible.

The result is a film that feels remarkably grounded despite its interstellar setting. Gosling brings warmth, humour, and vulnerability to the role of Ryland Grace, whilst Sandra Hüller delivers a commanding turn as the formidable Eva Stratt. The portrayal of Rocky, brought to life by James Ortiz, has become one of the most beloved characters in recent cinema.

The film was shot entirely in England, with principal photography running from June to October 2024 at Shepperton Studios, South Parade Pier in Portsmouth, and the Mullard Radio Astronomy Observatory near Cambridge.
