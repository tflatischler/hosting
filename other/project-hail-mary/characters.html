Project Hail Mary

Characters

The people — and beings — at the centre of humanity's most desperate mission.

Dr Ryland Grace

Reluctant Astronaut · Molecular Biologist

Ryland Grace is, by his own admission, nobody's idea of an astronaut. He is a molecular biologist who left the world of academic research behind to become a school science teacher. He loves teaching, he loves his students, and he has absolutely no desire to leave Earth.

Yet when a mysterious threat puts the entire planet in danger, Ryland finds himself at the centre of the response — not because he volunteered, but because his specific knowledge makes him essential. Waking up alone on a spaceship with no memory of how he got there, he must rely on the very skills he once used in the classroom: patience, curiosity, and a deep belief in the scientific method.

What makes Ryland so engaging as a character is his ordinariness. He is frightened, confused, and occasionally clumsy. But he is also stubborn, remarkably clever, and capable of extraordinary kindness under impossible pressure.

Film: played by Ryan Gosling

Rocky

Eridian Engineer · Interstellar Ally

Rocky is, without a doubt, one of the most memorable characters in modern science fiction. An alien from the 40 Eridani star system, Rocky is utterly unlike any human — and yet the bond he forms with Ryland becomes the emotional heart of the entire novel.

Rocky does not see the world through sight; instead, he perceives his surroundings through an incredibly refined sense of sound. He communicates by producing musical chords and tones, which makes the early stages of his relationship with Ryland both challenging and deeply fascinating.

As an engineer, Rocky is extraordinarily talented. He can build and repair almost anything, often using materials and methods that human scientists have never even imagined. But beyond his technical abilities, Rocky is loyal, brave, and surprisingly emotional. His friendship with Ryland is built on mutual respect, shared purpose, and a willingness to trust someone completely different from oneself.

Film: played by James Ortiz

Eva Stratt

Project Leader · International Authority

Eva Stratt is the woman in charge of saving the world — and she takes this responsibility with an intensity that is both admirable and unsettling. As the leader of the Hail Mary project, she has been granted authority by every nation on Earth, making her arguably the most powerful person alive.

Stratt is fiercely intelligent, brutally direct, and entirely willing to make morally questionable decisions if she believes they will increase humanity's chances of survival. She does not ask for permission and she rarely apologises. In many ways, she represents the difficult question at the core of the story: how far should we go to protect the greater good?

Despite her tough exterior, Stratt is not a villain. She carries the weight of her choices, and the novel gives us enough glimpses of her private moments to understand that the burden of leadership is enormous.

Film: played by Sandra Hüller

The Supporting Cast

Scientists · Engineers · Leaders

The novel features a rich cast of supporting characters who appear mainly through Ryland's flashback memories. These include scientists from around the world, military figures, engineers, and ordinary people drawn into an extraordinary situation.

Each one contributes something essential — whether it is a crucial piece of scientific knowledge, a bold engineering solution, or simply the human perspective that reminds us what is truly at stake. Together, they represent the idea that saving the world is never a one-person effort; it requires cooperation across borders, disciplines, and differences.
